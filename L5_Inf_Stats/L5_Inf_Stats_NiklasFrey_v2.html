Use descriptive statistics and a statistical test to analyze the Stroop effect¶

The input data is available here: https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B9Yf01UaIbUgQXpYb2NhZ29yX1U/view

Question 1: Identify variables in the experiment¶

Answer 1:
Independent variable: Is the colour Congruent or Incongruent?
Dependent variable: Average time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists

Question 2a: Establish hypotheses¶

What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task?

Question 2b: Establish a statistical test¶

What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.

Answer 2a:
Null hypotheses H0: H0: 1 = 2, where 1 is the population mean time for Congruent group, and 2 is the population mean time for Incongruent group
Null hypotheses in words: The incongruent color will results in the same population mean time as congruent.

Alternative hypotheses H1: H1: 1 2, where 1 is the mean of sampling 1, and 2 is the mean of sampling 2
Alternative hypotheses in words: The incongruent color changes the population mean time to name the ink color.

Answer 2b:
Statistical Test : dependent t-test (two tailed)
Justification:

  • We do not know pouplation parameters.
  • Pre Test / Post Test study
  • We want to compare two related groups to determine the statistically significant difference between two means.
  • Assumption: Normal distribution
  • Number of samples is less than 30
  • Two tailed, since hypothesis is testing equality of two means

from scipy import stats
DOF = 23
stats.t.ppf(0.975, DOF)

# Calculate the T-test on TWO RELATED samples of scores, a and b.
stats.ttest_rel(df1['Congruent'], df1['Incongruent'])
Out[41]:
Ttest_relResult(statistic=-8.020706944109957, pvalue=4.1030005857111781e-08)

Answer 5:¶

Since the p-value is clearly below 0.05, we reject the Null Hypthesis.
As a conclusion, we have evidence that the incongruent color changes the average time to name the ink color.
